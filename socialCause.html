No War:

There are wars breaking out in some parts of the world, thereby forcing millions of people to flee from their homes. Families are losing their beloved ones. In general, children and women are the ones hardest hit by the war, causing them trauma for the rest of their lives. After spending years in the refugee camps, the children, who are the future generation of the country, are in despair, losing proper education and healthcare. Wars bring loss of life, trauma, and displacement. In addition, it causes ecological damage, including deforestation and pollution. Therefore, war is not the option to resolve conflicts between parties or countries, but a peaceful approach is. Pursuing peace is crucial for safeguarding human rights and dignity, and it also contributes to global stability and reduces the risk of widespread crises.
